# Rule Name Category Sub-Type How It Works Origin / Source
1 1-4-7 Rule Learning & Memory Spaced Repetition Review new info on Day 1, Day 4, and Day 7 after learning it. Forces recall just before you forget, locking concepts into long-term memory. Ebbinghaus Forgetting Curve
2 Feynman Technique Learning & Memory Conceptual Understanding Learn a concept, then explain it simply as if teaching a beginner. Where you stumble = your knowledge gap. Go back and study that part. Richard Feynman
3 80/20 Rule Learning & Memory Pareto Principle 80% of results come from 20% of efforts. Focus on mastering core principles first before expanding to peripheral details. Vilfredo Pareto
4 Spaced Repetition (SRS) Learning & Memory Memory Optimisation Algorithm-backed revision system. Difficult items appear sooner, easy items recede. Maximises retention per minute of study. Ebbinghaus / Anki
5 20-20-20 Rule Focus & Sessions Eye Health Every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds. Prevents eye strain and mental fatigue during long screen sessions. American Optometric Association
6 Pomodoro Technique Focus & Sessions Time Boxing Work for 25 minutes uninterrupted, then 5-minute break. After 4 cycles, take a 15–30 min long break. Creates urgency and prevents burnout. Francesco Cirillo
7 90-Minute Ultradian Rule Focus & Sessions Biological Rhythm Work in 90-minute deep focus cycles aligned with your brain's ultradian rhythm, then rest 10–20 min. Matches biology to maximise performance. Nathaniel Kleitman
8 2-Minute Rule Productivity Task Management If a task takes less than 2 minutes, do it immediately. Stops small tasks from accumulating and creating mental clutter. David Allen — Getting Things Done
9 5-Second Rule Productivity Anti-Procrastination Count 5-4-3-2-1 and physically move when you have an instinct to act. Interrupts overthinking and hesitation before starting a hard task. Mel Robbins
10 Eat the Frog Productivity Priority Management Do your hardest or most dreaded task first each morning when willpower peaks. Everything after feels easier. Brian Tracy
11 Parkinson's Law Productivity Deadline Management Work expands to fill the time allotted. Set tight, intentional deadlines to force prioritisation and cut perfectionism-driven bloat. Cyril Northcote Parkinson
12 1-3-5 Rule Productivity Daily Planning Plan each day with 1 big task, 3 medium tasks, and 5 small tasks. Keeps your list realistic and forces you to prioritise rather than overcommit. Alex Cavoulacos
13 Active Recall Study Techniques Retrieval Practice Close your notes and force yourself to recall information — flashcards, practice questions, or blank-page recall. Far more effective than re-reading. Cognitive Psychology Research
14 Cornell Note Method Study Techniques Note-Taking System Divide page into 3 zones: narrow left column for keywords/questions, wide right for notes, bottom strip for summary. Review by covering notes and using cues to recall. Walter Pauk, Cornell University
15 Interleaving Study Techniques Mixed Practice Alternate between different subjects or topics in one session instead of blocking one subject for hours. Feels harder but builds deeper, more flexible understanding. Robert Bjork — Cognitive Science
16 Mind Mapping Study Techniques Visual Organisation Start with a central concept and branch out with related ideas, subtopics, and connections. Engages both visual and logical thinking for complex topics. Tony Buzan
17 The SQ3R Method Study Techniques Reading Comprehension Survey → Question → Read → Recite → Review. A structured approach to reading textbooks that turns passive reading into active learning. Francis P. Robinson
18 Blurting Method Study Techniques Brain Dump Recall Read a topic, close the book, and write down everything you remember on a blank page. Then check what you missed and repeat. Rapidly exposes gaps. Popular Exam Technique
19 Elaborative Interrogation Study Techniques Deep Understanding Ask 'Why is this true?' and 'How does this connect to what I already know?' for every new fact. Forces deeper processing beyond surface memorisation. Educational Psychology Research
20 Chunking Study Techniques Information Grouping Break large amounts of information into smaller, meaningful groups or patterns. Makes complex material easier to hold in working memory and recall later. George Miller — Cognitive Psychology
